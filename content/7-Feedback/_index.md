> Below are my personal opinions and feedback regarding my experience participating in the First Cloud AI Journey (FCAJ) program. I hope this helps the FCAJ team continue to improve this amazing bootcamp!

### Overall Evaluation

**1. Working Environment**  
The learning and working environment at FCAJ is incredibly open and dynamic. The community on the Facebook group and Discord channel is always active, and members are eager to help whenever I encounter AWS configuration or coding issues. This collaborative environment helped me stay focused and motivated throughout the 12 weeks.

**2. Support from Team Admin**  
The team admin provided exceptional support. Instead of just teaching theory, they always provided practical examples when explaining difficult concepts (like VPC, ALB, Serverless). When my team faced deployment issues with the Money Manager project, the mentor encouraged us to debug the CloudWatch logs ourselves before pointing out the solution, which drastically improved my troubleshooting skills.

**3. Relevance of Work to Academic Major**  
The curriculum and the final project (Money Manager) were highly relevant to my Information Technology major. While university gave me a strong foundation in programming, FCAJ bridged the gap by teaching me how to design, deploy, and secure a highly available application on a real cloud platform.

**4. Learning & Skill Development Opportunities**  
This bootcamp was a massive accelerator for my career. Beyond just learning AWS services, I gained hands-on experience with modern practices like CI/CD, writing AWS CloudFormation/SAM templates, and working in an Agile team. These are practical skills that are highly sought after in the industry.

**5. Company Culture & Team Spirit**  
The FCAJ community culture is overwhelmingly positive. Even though it was a bootcamp, it felt like a real tech company. Everyone respected each other's ideas during our project architecture design phases. The teamwork during the intense final weeks of the Money Manager project was a highlight of my experience.

**6. Internship Policies / Benefits**  
Having access to well-structured workshops, free AWS credits/resources for learning, and a community of like-minded peers are fantastic benefits. The program’s flexibility allowed me to balance my university studies while completing the rigorous weekly worklogs.

---

### Additional Feedback

**- What did you find most satisfying during your internship?**  
Successfully deploying the Money Manager application with a fully functioning Multi-AZ database and Auto Scaling group. Seeing the architecture we designed actually work and handle traffic was incredibly rewarding.

**- What do you think the company should improve for future interns?**  
It would be great to have a few more interactive Q&A sessions focused specifically on debugging common AWS errors. Sometimes beginners get stuck on IAM permissions or Security Group rules for days.

**- If recommending to a friend, would you suggest they intern here? Why or why not?**  
Absolutely. FCAJ provides a rare combination of structured learning, hands-on AWS practice, and an incredibly supportive community. It is the perfect launchpad for anyone wanting to become a Cloud Engineer.

---

### Suggestions & Expectations
- **Do you have any suggestions to improve the internship experience?**  
Perhaps incorporating a mini-hackathon halfway through the program to help students bond and practice their skills under pressure.
- **Would you like to continue this program in the future?**  
Yes, I would love to return as a mentor or participate in advanced FCAJ programs focusing on AI/ML or DevOps.
